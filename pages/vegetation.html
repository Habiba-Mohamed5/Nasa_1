The Story of Vegetation

A journey through two decades of change in Earth's green cover, as seen by NASA's Terra satellite.

From Green to Brown

At the turn of the millennium, the Amazon rainforest, often called the "lungs of the planet," was a vast, uninterrupted sea of green. Data from the MODIS instrument aboard the Terra satellite provided an unprecedented baseline of global vegetation health, measured by the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI).

However, as the years progressed, a worrying trend began to emerge. Between 2005 and 2012, increased deforestation for agriculture and logging began to leave visible scars on the landscape. The NDVI data shows a consistent decline during this period, indicating stressed or disappearing vegetation.

The Tipping Point

The period from 2013 onwards marked a critical acceleration in vegetation loss. This coincided with an increase in large-scale wildfires, another phenomenon tracked by Terra. The data tells a clear story: what was once a resilient ecosystem is now under severe threat, impacting global climate patterns and biodiversity.

Key Facts

  • Instrument: MODIS
  • Measurement: NDVI
  • Key Finding: Steady decline post-2005.
