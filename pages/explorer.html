A Forest's Breath

The story of the Amazon rainforest through two decades of NASA's Terra data.

2000-2005: A Stable Green Expanse

The new millennium began with the Amazon appearing as a vast, healthy ecosystem. Data from MODIS established a baseline for the planet's largest rainforest.

2006-2012: The First Scars

This period marked the beginning of a noticeable decline due to deforestation pressures, fragmenting the forest edges.

2013-2018: Accelerated Decline

The rate of vegetation loss increased significantly, coinciding with more frequent and intense large-scale wildfires.

2019-2023: A System Under Threat

Recent data shows the Amazon approaching a critical tipping point, threatening global climate patterns and biodiversity.
